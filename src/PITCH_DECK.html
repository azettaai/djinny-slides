02 · PROBLEM

The creator can't sell it. The enterprise can't trust it. Same reason.

Frontier models dominate because no team can justify training at that scale — even Apple pays Google, OpenAI, or Anthropic. Local models are the answer: full ownership, end-to-end security, deep fine-tuning specialization. But distributing local model weights is broken the same way for both sides of the market.

01 · THE CREATOR

Six months of fine-tuning. Zero ways to sell it.

You've trained a specialized model on proprietary data. You want to monetize it. Your options collapse to two:

  • Hide it behind an API. You operate the infra. Buyers get a prompt box — no local deployment, no offline, no fine-tuning on their data. Same limitations as the frontier models they're already paying for, now at your cost.
  • Post it free on HuggingFace. Pirated within hours. Zero revenue, zero attribution — and your training corpus is now publicly recoverable from the weights.

Every commercial deal that avoids these two exits requires months of litigation-heavy sales calls negotiating licensing, privacy, and IP before a contract exists. There is no self-serve. There is no App Store. The market for selling a local model does not function.

02 · THE ENTERPRISE

Local AI is the requirement. Nothing on the market is safe to deploy.

Auto OEMs, medical devices, smartphones, AR headsets — these products need on-device AI. Frontier API models are a non-starter: data leaves the network, the vendor controls capability, offline is impossible.

  • Open weights on-device. Weights at rest on a device you ship are weights you've surrendered. Anyone who gets the device gets the model — and anyone who gets the model gets the training data baked into it.
  • Roll your own DRM. No cross-vendor standard exists. Years of per-platform engineering, with no guarantee regulators accept the result.

UNECE WP.29, EU AI Act, FDA high-risk AI — regulators are requiring model provenance the same way they require supply-chain provenance. The compliance infrastructure doesn't exist yet. Neither does the security standard.

03 · THE ROOT CAUSE

Weights that leave your server unprotected are weights you've lost.

Model weights are not a binary blob. They are the training corpus compressed into parameters. Carlini et al. (2021) extracted memorized PII and full training samples from GPT-2 by querying the model. Fine-tunes inherit proprietary documents, customer chats, and internal codebases directly into the weight space — the richer the model, the worse the breach.

The creator loses IP and revenue. The enterprise inherits a data breach in every device it ships. Both are blocked by the same missing primitive: a way to hand someone model weights without handing them the rights to those weights. That primitive does not exist. Distribution economics compound the problem — hosting and transferring weights at scale builds egress bills in the millions. HuggingFace absorbs this as a subsidy. No paid model can.

One gap. Both markets. You cannot distribute model weights to a device you don't control while retaining cryptographic rights to them. That single missing capability is why creators cannot build a business selling local models, and why enterprises cannot safely deploy them. Same problem. One solution.

03 · SECURITY_PREMISE

Open weights are a data breach waiting to happen.

Model weights aren't a binary blob. They are the training data, compressed into parameters. Anyone who obtains the weights can extract what was trained into them — and this isn't speculative. It's the published consensus across three independent lines of research.

01 · EXTRACTION

Training data is recoverable from weights

Carlini et al. (2021) extracted memorized training samples — including PII, code, full URLs — from GPT-2 by querying the model. Subsequent work has scaled the technique to LLaMA-class and production fine-tunes.

02 · LOCATION

Knowledge is locatable in parameters

ROME (Meng et al. 2022) and MEMIT (Meng et al. 2023) demonstrate that specific facts live in identifiable weight regions and can be edited in place. Azetta's own research on knowledge editing of neural networks confirms the obverse: if you can edit it, you can read it.

03 · FINE-TUNES

Custom-trained models leak the most

Fine-tunes inherit proprietary documents, customer chats, internal codebases, and PII directly into the weight space. Distributing the weights = distributing the training set. The richer the model, the worse the breach.

The implication. Open-weight distribution is the security analog of publishing your database. Periodica's protocol isn't piracy protection — it's data-leak protection. We built it because our research convinced us this is where on-device AI security is heading: regulators will require model-weight confidentiality the same way they require database confidentiality today.

04 · SOLUTION

One answer for both sides of the market.

Because the creator's distribution problem and the enterprise's security problem share the same root cause, they share the same solution: encrypted, watermarked, revocable weights — distributed to any device — with cryptographic rights that stay with the issuer. That is Periodica. Sell a model the way Apple sells an app. Deploy it the way regulators require.

For creators

  • DRM that actually works. Models distributed encrypted; license keys revoke on cancellation. Per-customer watermarking traces every leak back to the licensee.
  • Self-serve marketplace billing. Subscriptions, one-off purchases, revenue share — no sales call required. Stripe handles the transaction layer.
  • Verified namespace. Brand protection from day one; impersonators blocked at the registry level.

For enterprises and OEMs

  • Encrypted fleet deployment. Weights at rest on every device, per-device key binding, server-side revocation that actually revokes. Cars, phones, AR headsets, medical devices — cross-vendor, one SDK.
  • Audit pipeline + provenance. Four-tier audit: L1 auto-scan through L4 Periodica Certified. Regulatory-grade lineage, capability radar, fine-tune delta.
  • BYOK + on-prem license server. Data never leaves the enterprise network. Meets UNECE WP.29, EU AI Act, FDA high-risk AI requirements by construction.

08 · REGULATORY / FORCING_FUNCTIONS

The buyer can't say no.

Four mandatory frameworks active or imminent in 2026. Procurement language in regulated verticals already requires documented model provenance.

EU AI ACT

High-risk class

Enforcement starts Aug 2, 2026. Detailed technical documentation, conformity assessment, CE marking, EU database registration. Article 50 effectively mandates C2PA-style content credentials.

~3 months out. Procurement RFPs already requiring "documented model provenance."

UNECE WP.29

R155 / R156 (automotive)

CSMS + SUMS mandatory for all new vehicles sold in EU / Japan / Korea since July 2024. Type-approval bodies are denying approvals for non-compliant vehicles.

Already biting. VW Group, Stellantis, Bosch publicly compliant.

FDA

Premarket cybersecurity guidance

Updated March 2026. SBOM mandatory under FD&C Act §524B. FDA has rejected 510(k) / PMA submissions for inadequate cybersecurity. PCCPs require documented validation.

Active enforcement. Model-BOM extension is the natural fit.

US POLICY

Model-theft category

April 2026: State Department cable on DeepSeek / Moonshot / MiniMax distillation theft. Stop AI Model Theft Act (Huizenga). Frontier Model Forum joint intelligence sharing.

First time the US government treats AI model IP as critical infrastructure.

ISO/IEC 42001 (AI Management System) certifications grew 20% in 2024 — becoming a procurement check-box. Periodica's audit pipeline maps to AIMS Clause 6.1 directly. Pitch language: "we make compliance cheaper and verifiable" — not "we are compliance". Honest framing wins DD.
